INFORMATION ABOUT COVID-19 (CORONAVIRUS)

WHAT IS COVID-19?

COVID-19, otherwise known as coronavirus disease 2019, is a new infectious disease that affects your lungs and airways.

What are the symptoms of Covid-19?

Most people who feel ill with COVID-19 will have at least one of the key symptoms:
  1. Fever
  2. Dry Cough
  3. Triedness
  4. Loss of taste or smell
Other symptoms include:
  1. Nasal Congestion
  2. Conjunctivitis (Red Eyes)
  3. Sore throat
  4. Headache
  5. Muscle of joint pain
  6. Skin rash
  7. Vomiting
  8. Diarrhoea
  9. Dizziness
Some people get more seriously ill with COVID-19 and may have symptoms such as:
  1. Shortness of breath
  2. Loss of appetite
  3. Chest pain
  4. High temperature
These people will need medical care. In severe cases, COVID-19 can lead to death. COVID-19 affects different people in different ways:
Some people do not have any symptoms and may not even know they have the virus.
You can still pass COVID-19 on even if you aren’t showing any symptoms, so it’s important to always follow the prevention advice.

How to prevent the spread of germs when caring for someone who is sick?

  1. Have the person stay in one room, away from other people, including yourself, as much as possible.
  2. If possible, have them use a separate bathroom.
  3. Avoid sharing personal household items, like dishes, towels, and bedding
  4. Have them wear a cloth face covering (that covers their nose and mouth) when they are around people, including you.
  5. If the sick person can’t wear a cloth face covering, you should wear one while in the same room with them.
  6. If the sick person needs to be around others (within the home, in a vehicle, or doctor’s office), they should wear a cloth face covering that covers their mouth and nose.
  7. Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, especially after interacting with the sick person. If soap and water are not readily available, use a hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol. Cover all surfaces of your hands and rub them together until they feel dry.
  8. Avoid touching your eyes, nose, and mouth.
